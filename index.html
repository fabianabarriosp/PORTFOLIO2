Learning Outcomes

LO1: Interactive Media Products

You orient in the relevant tech, media, and design landscape and create interactive media products that you have tested with users and stakeholders.

Portfolio - Figma Blik - Pre User Testing Monk - Research Proposal & Interview Questions Monk - Instagram Mock-up Monk - Pre User Testing Monk - User Testing

I learned how to combine tech and design skills to create interactive products and test them with users, which was helpful because it taught me the importance of user feedback and collaboration, making my work more effective and efficient.

LO2: Development & Version Control

You explore front-end development languages, write code, and document in a version control environment.

GIT - Portfolio Magazine GIT - Fiddle Exercise GIT - Final Portfolio

This LO was helpful because it taught me how to keep my code organized and track changes, especially with Git. I realized the importance of code structure and comments to keep myself up to date with the changes I make and continue to work on.

LO3: Iterative Design

You explore and use professional design tools and iteratively design visual works.

Portfolio - Initial Ideas Portfolio - Figma Designs Blik - Figma Designs Monk - Posters

This LO emphasized the value of using professional design tools and having an iterative approach to creating visual products. It taught me how essential it is to refine designs over time, testing and improving them based on feedback from others. Iterations have helped me develop more effective designs, sharpening my skills in both creativity and technical execution.

LO4: Professional Standard

You apply professional practice, both individually and in teams, in the areas of project organization, communication with stakeholders, exploratory research, and reporting.

Blik - Initial Presentation Blik - Final Presentation Blik - Team Charter Monk - Initial Presentation Monk - Final Presentation Monk - Team Charter Monk - Research Proposal Monk - Content Strategy

This LO reinforced how essential it is to organize projects effectively, communicate clearly with stakeholders and group members, and conduct thorough research. This has been crucial for my development, as it improved my ability to collaborate with others and contribute meaningfully to projects.

LO5: Personal Leadership

You take the initiative in asking for and reflecting on feedback. You identify your own core values as the basis for your study career and professional development.

Feedpulse Blik - Trello Monk 1 - Trello Monk 2 - Trello Belbin Workshop Core Values Workshop

I realized the importance of taking initiative in seeking and reflecting on feedback to keep track of my work and have other people's perspectives on it. Also, identifying my core values helped me clarify what aspects I still need to work on and to focus on them.
