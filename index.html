About

This project is to showcase the work I've done throughout the semester like my technical skills, such as web development, brand design and multimedia content creation. It's used to document the projects I've worked on like Branding theater Blik, Media Campaign for Monk, Project X and allows me to track my progress over time.

1st & 2nd Iteration

I made different designs, one more journal-looking and another one with a magazine style. The magazine design felt more elegant and aligned with the professional tone I wanted for my portfolio. I chose a picture of myself from two different angles: one for the cover page (front view) and one for the end page (back view). The idea was to symbolize the journey and progression throughout the projects, where the front-facing image introduces me and my work, and the back-facing image marks the conclusion.

Tools

I used tools like Affinity Design, Figma and Pinterest to create my portfolio. Pinterest to help me gather my ideas which I later envisioned in AD and created a prototype in Figma.

3rd Iteration

I chose a black-and-white minimalistic design for my portfolio to keep the focus on my projects and make everything easy to read and navigate. It’s simple but professional, which helps showcase my work without distractions. This approach also allowed me to finish the portfolio quickly and meet the deadline while leaving room to add small improvements later. It shows that I can prioritize and adapt when needed.

Feedback

I was told the idea of making my portfolio like a magazine and adding the animation to flip the pages was really cool, although navigation would be interesting to see what i would come up with. At first i was planning on creating an index page but soon realized it was not ideal as the user would have to scroll through all the pages and cannot easily access a specific section.

Reflection

Creating my portfolio has been an important learning experience. I initially aimed for an interactive, magazine-style design with animations to showcase my creativity. However, after struggling for weeks, I reassessed my priorities and shifted to a minimalist black-and-white style, focusing on clarity and functionality. This decision taught me the value of adaptability and purpose over perfection. The portfolio also allowed me to reflect on my progress and understand how to present my work professionally, helping me realize the importance of organizing information and storytelling in my career. Moving forward, I plan to refine the design while keeping it clean and effective.

About

We were assigned to re-brand Theater Blik, four women aiming to expand their little improv group in Amersfoort into an association of actors and musicians. Our mission was to create a brand identity for the Blik group that fits their goals and personality. Additionally, provide useful tips on how to update different social media platforms to promote their work around The Netherlands.

Mood-Board

I gathered images from Pinterest and created a mood-board to conceptualize the overall vibe of the brand. I combined images, text and symbols for this.


Stylescape

Before getting feedback from the client, i created a stylescape to give them a better concept of more concrete ideas by visualizing possible logos, typography & color palette.

Feedback

The clients did not enjoy the colors proposed although they commented how i chose yellow to evoke happiness and brown to relate to the growth of roots and connections in trees. As a group we ended up presenting the client with a darker range of colors creating an elegant and minimalistic atmosphere

Documentation

I made a document including the Team Charter, Project Plan stating the different tasks we had to have done by the end of the week. This step was crucial for the project as it kept us organized and it helped us as a group be held accountable for meetings, individual tasks and responsabilities

Presentation 1

Halfway through the project, we had in-person contact with our clients and I created a presentation of our progress so far and what we were planning on working on in the following 2 weeks.


Visuals

I thought our designing for BLIK needed a little pop of color, and created squiggly lines that would add some playfulness. The lines are undefined and have no specific path but connect when needed, just like the people that compose Blik.

Presentation 2

Adeline and I presented our group's final deliverables, and the rest of the group joined later for a round of questions/feedback from the client.


Content Strategy

I created a simple social media post for our clients to start visualizing the format in which we would later deliver the products.

Reflection

Although my design wasn't the final one we presented to the Blik representatives I still played a role in creating the final deliverables. Because the Blik ladies wanted a playful style for their brand I came up with the squiggly lines that represent an abstract path, because Blik is an improvisation theater group, the audience (and even the actors themselves) never know where they´re heading. The squiggly lines are undefined and do not cross but eventually connect when its needed, just like people.

About

We had to create a media campaign for Monkshop and place them as leading experts in bouldering. Before creating visuals for our Campaign I created a research proposal to help us figure out how to approach the marketing of Monkshop.

1st Iteration

"It’s not you, It’s our gear" is a slogan i came up with to emphasize that Monkshop takes responsibility for providing top-quality gear. I wanted to imply that if their climb isn’t going well, it’s the fault of their equipment and not their abilities. We presented our ideas to the clients and they mentioned it came off as dismissive rather than encouraging. So I changed the tone of the next iteraations.

2nd Iteration

The message changes to "Our expertise, Your climb," shifting focus from taking responsibility for the gear to a more collaborative relationship between Monkshop and the climber. I made "Your" bold to empower the climber and make it about them. I changed the monk logo to yellow as its associated with feelings of optimism, energy, and confidence and to create a more cohesive and relaxing feel when looking at the image.

3rd Iteration

Clients didn't enjoy the first designs so I used the same tagline but emphasizing “Your” in a colorful and playful way to simulate the colored rocks when practicing bouldering inside. Monk aims to have positive encouragement with its customers so the over-sized, colorful letters in "Your" immediately draw attention to the message that Monk’s products and expertise are focused on empowering the climber's personal journey (in bouldering and outside). Some people may not relate the letters with the holds, which come in different shapes, sizes, and colors to create routes with various difficulty levels and styles for climbers and that´s the reason I specifically chose this image for the background of the poster.

Brain-Storming

We presented again our ideas to the client and didn't approve any of them. We brainstormed possible concepts to focus our campaign on. The final 2 ideas that made the cut were “Whats your excuse” and “Be”, both encouraging.

4th Iteration

My interpretation of this tagline is that Monkshop is trying to encourage its customers to finally confront and overcome challenges that have been holding them back, specifically to embrace the difficulties that come with bouldering but also in their personal lives, evoking emotions in the target audience so they feel closer to Monk. In particular, I created these posters to encourage everyone to start bouldering, as age is just a number, focus on the gear (provided by Monk) and to seek discomfort in trying new things instead of laying down. Bold big letters to make the tagline standout.
My focus was in creating positive feelings of encouragement, as opposed to my first iterations, to reflects the brand’s commitment to their growth and fosters trust and loyalty with Monk.

Feedback

Clients told me the first designs are original designs, eye-catchy but they deviate from Monk and I should focus on promoting the actual shop because these specifically seem like their message is to target a broader audience.

5th Iteration

I iterated on my designs to focus on products sold. Specifically to highlight that Monk provides high-quality gear suitable for climbers of all ages and abilities, provides right gear without the burden of carrying excess or ineffective equipment, and to motivate climbers to choose MonkShop equipment that encourages them to get off the couch and climb.

Mock-ups

Using the 2 final concepts I created Instagram mock-ups to portray how their social media content could look like.
Reflection

This project taught me the importance of tone and the value of audience-centered messaging, and to really cater the content you are creating to the brands identity and style. Also communication with the client is not always as smooth as yput think it will go.

Considering the layout principles we created a 2 page magazine. There were certain requirements from a fictional client that limited our creativity when organizing the content.Later, I combined what I learned with a development workshop where we were given a basic HTML structure and had to design it so that it looks like a real newspaper.

Design Principles

Considering layout principles arrange content (text, images, headings) in a visually appealing and functional way, reflecting current design standards in media.

Documentation

I opened a couple existing newspaper sites to see the overall structure and get some inspiration . I commented on my code explaining functions and layouts to make my work understandable to others (and to my future self).

Feedback

Pay attention when im writing as several times ive gotten stuck a long time on silly spelling mistakes/structure and waste time trying to fix it.

Reflection

With the layout exercise I learned the importance of taking your time into planning as each member of the group started cutting shapes with no sense of direction and ended up wasting time and materials. After I made a rough draft of the content I cut out the shapes and we were able to create the magazine. As for the Fiddle exercise, I feel this was a must-do exercise as it refreshed my memory after a long summer as it allowed me to re-gain certain knowledge like shortcuts to save time. I now realize how helpful commenting on code is for easier editing later on. This specific task was very beneficial to my portfolio project as it really made me practice with grid layout.

About

Using the layout principles from the fiddle exercise workshop I designed and printed a magazine for my dads birthday.
Why a Magazine?

There are 2 main reasons that led me to create this magazine. First, I was stuck on what to get him for his birthday because he has his own money and could generaly buy anything he wanted. Aditionally, I was feeling overwhelmed because my portfolio magazine hadnt worked out and figured I could use my Media Design knowledge for a real life scenario.

Brainstorming

Before I started to design the pages, I brainstormed ideas to organize myself as I had a minimum of 28 to be able to print it.

1st & 2nd Iteration

In the first iteration, the bold, all-caps serif font and bright green background emphasizes the page and captures attention immediately. In the second, the Beau Rivage font gives an elegant touch, but I mainly chose it because it looks like my dads handwriting and I wanted to make it seem like he had written that (because its his recipe). I used a modern sans-serif for the actual "recipe" body text in both versions, to make sure that the instructions are easy to read and follow.

3rd & 4th Iteration

I chose a bright golden yellow background that I thought captured his warmth and energy. I added arrows for a dynamic layout and to give like a sense of movement with the background lines to guide the eye through his favorite items in an organized way.

4th & 5th Iteration

I was inspired by the classic “Guess Who?” game, it’s a playful and interactive way for him to guess who’s who in these baby pictures. I went with bold reds and blues to make the page feel fun and vibrant, like in the original game.

Reflection

I am very proud of the final result of the magazine.I learned a lot of things from the ICT & Media Design profile and Im glad I could use the skills I learned to practice in a real life scenario. I believe I really encapsultades my dads personality but still added my own touch to it.

About

We identified 3-4 of our own behavioral attributes of Meredith Belbins research and we later proceeded to match 1 to each of our team members. This helped us recognize in a deeper level our strengths and weaknesses as a group to be able to work better together. At the beginning of our first project I created a team charter to set a general idea of the team members to help us exploit our strengths.
After identifying our own Belbin attributes, we shared them with each other and mostly everyone was in the same symphony.

Reflection

Reflecting on my strengths and weaknesses, for upcoming projects we can organize ourselves better as a group and not only assign tasks we’re good at but the opposite so we can develop our skills on that as well.

LO2: Development & Version Control

You explore and use professional design tools and iteratively design visual works.
